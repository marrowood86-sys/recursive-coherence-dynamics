Recursive Coherence Dynamics
OverviewRecursive Coherence Dynamics (RCD) is a computational research framework exploring the emergence of low-dimensional spectral organization in adaptive, co-evolving graphs. The model demonstrates that a stable, low-dimensional spectral attractor can emerge from simple dual-feedback loops—specifically, the interaction between phase-synchronization engines and localized entropy regulation—without requiring embedded spatial coordinates or externally imposed geometric constraints. 
Project StatusThis repository serves as the official companion to the white paper, "Recursive Coherence Dynamics: Emergence of Robust Low-Dimensional Spectral Organization in Adaptive Graphs". The project has transitioned from an exploratory simulation to a rigorous empirical study of emergent order parameters in network topology.  Key FindingsGenerative Asymmetry: Ablation studies confirm that phase alignment acts as the primary generative mechanism for structural condensation, while entropy regulation serves as the essential stabilizing brake preventing structural runaway.  Finite-Size Scaling: Simulations across scales ($N=32$ to $N=512$) demonstrate that the emergent spectral attractor is robust. We observe a dramatic "variance collapse" as $N$ increases, indicating convergence toward a well-defined large-system limit.  Emergent Order Parameter: We interpret the spectral dimension ($d_s$) not as a fixed universal constant, but as a macroscopic order parameter describing the collective organization of the adaptive graph.  Structural Basins of Attraction: Initialization sensitivity analysis suggests the existence of multiple structural basins of attraction, motivating future study into the phase-transition behavior of adaptive graphs.  
Research Roadmap: Systematic Empirical Characterization
This project is currently undergoing a rigorous program of empirical validation to map the topological phase structure of the RCD model. Key experiments in progress include:RC-DIAG-004: Threshold-Dependence MappingObjective: Execute a controlled sparsification threshold sweep (25% to 85%) to isolate whether $d_s$ is a robust property of the graph dynamics or a function of the coarse-graining scale.  Initialization Sensitivity AnalysisObjective: Systematic exploration of how varying initial connectivity density influences the attractor’s final coordinate, with the goal of characterizing the model's structural basins of attraction.
Microscopic-to-Macroscopic DerivationObjective: Pursuit of a first-principles derivation connecting the microscopic update rules to the observed macroscopic attractor coordinates.  Extended Finite-Size ScalingObjective: Investigating the scaling behavior beyond $N=512$ to further verify convergence toward the large-system thermodynamic limit.
